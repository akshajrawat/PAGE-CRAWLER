Figured I would share a bit about my typing journey, as I am kind of an enthusiast when it comes to ergonomics.

First, would like to share typ.ing/ (made by ZSA, the keyboard people behind the Ergodox EZ, Moonlander, etc.) as my favorite place to test new layouts and type. Notably, they have an option to paste your own example text and various settings for programming examples. Their daily challenges get progressively harder throughout the week and are some good practice with the ramp up.

I swapped to an Ergodox-EZ when I started my first full time programming gig to invest in my long term joint health. After playing World of Warcraft (Runescape and Diablo 2 are other notable examples, which were where I learned to type) as a teenager for 16-hour days, I needed a way to not feel pain in my hands. My typing journey will hopefully evolve to a non-QWERTY layout in the future. Right now, I am happy with my current Moonlander setup. Thankfully, my hand pain is mostly gone nowadays. I do type with a fairly large tent on each side, because ideally our typing position should be as close to a resting position as possible. Like playing the piano, you don't want your wrists below your fingers. Unlike a piano, you can get split boards and tent them into a natural position!
